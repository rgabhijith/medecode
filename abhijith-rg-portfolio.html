Chapter I — The Product Building what didn't exist
What I built

The platform that
owns the category.

Family office technology was broken — fragmented, manual, and built for institutions that moved slowly. I saw a different future and built it. AtlasFive is now the defining platform in the space.

The Starting Point
A blank canvas.
A broken industry.
When I joined Eton Solutions, family office operations ran on spreadsheets, siloed custodian portals, and manual workflows that consumed entire teams. There was no product in the market that solved the full picture — from trade capture to reporting to compliance. So I built one.
The problem was real. The market was waiting.
The Architecture Principle
Built once.
Scaled infinitely.
From day one, every decision was made with scale in mind. Feature frameworks, data architecture, integration patterns — built modular so that what worked for one family office could be configured, deployed, and adopted by hundreds. The product scales because the foundations were right.
Product decisions made at zero define what's possible at scale.
Market Position
First in class.
Defining the standard.
AtlasFive today is not one of many wealth management platforms. It is the benchmark the industry measures itself against. When global family offices evaluate enterprise software, the conversation starts with what AtlasFive does — and whether alternatives can match it. That market position wasn't inherited. It was built, feature by feature, release by release, through relentless focus on what sophisticated wealth operators actually need.
The Operational Moat
Custodian depth.
Integration breadth.
True market dominance in wealth technology comes from integrations that are hard to replicate. Deep custodian relationships — Wells Fargo, Bessemer Trust, and beyond. Trade capture systems that handle the full lifecycle. Reporting infrastructure that runs across hundreds of entities simultaneously. Every integration strengthens the moat. Every workflow embedded deepens the switching cost.
The network of integrations is the product.
Chapter II — The Frontier AI before AI was the answer
AI & Intelligence
While the industry was still debating whether AI belonged in wealth management, I had already shipped it. Not a demo. Not a pilot. Agents. In production. Built on top of years of proprietary data and a framework engineered to support exactly this moment.
◈
The Framework Advantage
The reason we could ship AI Agents to end users wasn't luck — it was infrastructure. Years of deliberate product architecture created a data layer and integration framework that AI could be layered onto without rebuilding from scratch. Most wealth platforms can't say the same.
Architecture-first thinking → AI-ready by design
◇
Agents in the Workflow
Our AI Agents live inside the product — inside the workflows family office teams run every day. They don't require users to change behavior or learn new tools. The intelligence surfaces at the moment of decision, in the context where it's needed, without ceremony.
Embedded intelligence · Zero friction adoption
◉
Proprietary Data as Competitive Fuel
Our AI isn't running on generic models applied to generic data. It operates on years of curated, structured, validated wealth management data — portfolio compositions, custodian feeds, entity hierarchies, trade histories. That specificity is a moat no new entrant can close quickly.
Domain data depth → irreplaceable intelligence
▲
What Automation Actually Means
Automation in our context isn't about cutting headcount. It's about elevating what skilled wealth professionals spend their time on. Hours once consumed by reconciliation, report generation, and data validation now resolve autonomously — freeing operators to do the high-judgment work only they can do.
Hours → seconds · Human attention directed upward
◐
Beyond Feature Parity
Most platforms are racing to add AI features. We're already beyond that race. The question for us is not "can we add AI?" — it's "how do we deepen intelligence across every layer of the platform?" That's a fundamentally different strategic position to operate from.
Not catching up — setting the pace

The Next Frontier
The roadmap ahead is about autonomous operations — agents that don't just surface insights but coordinate actions across systems, entities, and time horizons. The foundation is built. The data is there. The platform is ready. What comes next will redefine expectations for what wealth software can do.

Chapter III — The Journey Every role was a classroom
The backstory
"You don't build a category-defining product
without first understanding the category
from every angle it can be broken."
Twelve years across financial data, risk analytics, ETF infrastructure, and capital markets operations. Each chapter added a layer of understanding that would eventually make the product possible — and make the builder irreplaceable.

This isn't a work history. It's the curriculum that preceded the breakthrough.
2022 — Present
Eton Solutions
The creation chapter.

This is where everything before converged. Armed with deep domain knowledge in wealth operations, capital markets data, and risk systems, I stepped into a product role with a clear mandate: build something the industry has never seen. AtlasFive grew from concept to category leader under this tenure. AI Agents were designed, built, and delivered to end users using the platform's own data infrastructure. The market caught up to what we'd already shipped.

2017 — 2022
IHS Markit · S&P Global
Learning the language of data.

Five years inside one of the world's most important financial data businesses taught a singular lesson: data quality is product quality. Leading strategy for global ETF and Index data products — managing composition feeds, corporate actions, and multi-asset class delivery for institutional clients worldwide — built the obsessive precision that later became a competitive advantage. You can't build AI on bad data. I learned what good data infrastructure looks like at the highest level.

2013 — 2017
Oracle Financial Services
Reading risk.
At institutional depth.

Blackrock Capital Market Operations. Duration, yield, NAV, portfolio risk across equities, fixed income, structured products, derivatives. This is where the financial intuition was built — not in classrooms but in the discipline of verifying prices against Reuters, Merrill Lynch, and JPMorgan and understanding what a wrong number costs a fund manager at 6am. Risk is not a feature. It is the foundation of every product decision that followed.

2012
Fidelity Investments
Where it began.

The beginning of a deliberate career in financial services — fixed income quality operations, data integrity, and the discipline of process ownership. A foundation of accountability that never left.
